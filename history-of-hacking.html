History of Hacking

1960s - Early Computer Exploration

The term “hacker” originally referred to creative programmers at universities like MIT who explored and improved computer systems.

1980s - Rise of Computer Crime

Personal computers and early networks led to the first famous hacking cases, viruses, and cybercrime investigations.

1990s - Internet Expansion

The growth of the internet increased both cybersecurity awareness and online attacks.

2000s to Today - Cybersecurity Era

Governments, companies, and ethical hackers now work to defend systems from cyber threats including ransomware and data breaches.

Positive and Negative Societal Impacts

Positive Impacts

  • Ethical hackers help organizations identify security weaknesses.
  • Cybersecurity research protects hospitals, banks, schools, and governments.
  • Security awareness helps people protect privacy and personal information.
  • Bug bounty programs reward responsible vulnerability reporting.

Negative Impacts

  • Cybercrime can steal money and personal data.
  • Ransomware attacks disrupt hospitals and businesses.
  • Misinformation and cyber espionage affect governments and elections.
  • Privacy violations can occur through hacking or surveillance misuse.

Safe Ethical Hacking Coding Example

Ethical hacking focuses on security testing and defense. The example below demonstrates a simple password strength checker.

function checkPasswordStrength(password) { if (password.length < 8) { return "Weak Password"; } const hasNumber = /\d/.test(password); const hasUpper = /[A-Z]/.test(password); const hasSpecial = /[!@#$%^&*]/.test(password); if (hasNumber && hasUpper && hasSpecial) { return "Strong Password"; } return "Moderate Password"; }

Notable Figures in Cybersecurity

Kevin Mitnick

Kevin Mitnick was once one of the most famous hackers in the world. After serving prison time, he later became a cybersecurity consultant and educator.

Edward Snowden

Edward Snowden revealed classified information about global surveillance programs in 2013 and sparked worldwide debates about privacy and digital rights.

Global Surveillance and Privacy

Modern technology allows governments and organizations to collect large amounts of digital information.
